Married share by age and education

Americans born from 1957 to 1964, tracked at ages 24, 34, 44, 54, and 58. The chart shows the share married within each education group, with the total cohort as a reference line.

Age 24

Bachelor's 31% married

At age 24, the highest-education group was least likely to be married.

Age 58

Bachelor's 74% married

By age 58, the same group was most likely to be married, well above the cohort total of 64%.

A crossover by education

Higher education is associated with lower marriage rates at 24 but higher marriage rates from age 34 onward.

Source: U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics, The Economics Daily, September 18, 2025. Values shown from the provided dataset.
Chart logic: married share only; not-married is omitted because it is the direct complement to 100 percent.
