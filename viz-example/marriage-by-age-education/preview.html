BLS Marital Status Cohort

Higher education delayed marriage early, then led to higher marriage rates later

Among Americans born from 1957 to 1964, the most educated group was least likely to be married at age 24 but most likely to be married from age 34 onward. The strongest shift in the chart is not a steady cohort-wide climb, but a reversal in how marriage rates line up by education.

Takeaway: At age 24, only 31.1% of those with a bachelor's degree or higher were married, versus roughly half of those with a high school diploma or less. By age 58, the bachelor's group had the highest married share at 74.3%.

Scope: This is a cohort view for people born in 1957-64. The chart focuses on the married share because not-married is the direct complement to 100% in every row.

Source: U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics, The Economics Daily, September 18, 2025.
