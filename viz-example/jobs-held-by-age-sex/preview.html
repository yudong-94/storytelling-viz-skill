BLS Job Histories

Most job switching happened before age 25

Americans born from 1957 to 1964 held jobs at a much faster clip in their late teens and early twenties than in later adulthood. The age pattern dominates the story; the difference between men and women is comparatively small.

Takeaway: The cohort averaged 5.6 jobs before age 25, more than in any later age band, and men and women follow nearly the same downward pattern after that.

Scope: These are average jobs held within age buckets for the 1957-64 birth cohort in the National Longitudinal Survey of Youth 1979. Jobs spanning multiple age groups are counted in each age group, so the bucket values are not additive.

Source: U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics, The Economics Daily, September 26, 2025.
