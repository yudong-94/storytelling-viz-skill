Population versus household count across countries

Population is plotted against total households, with color encoding average people per household.

China and India are the clearest contrast: both are population giants, but China has far more households because its average household size is much smaller. Color encodes average people per household. Household distributions come from each country’s most recent available survey year, which varies by country.

How to read it

Think of population as width and households as height. Countries that sit unusually high for their width have smaller households; countries that sit lower bundle more people into each home.
